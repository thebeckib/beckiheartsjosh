Friends

January 2009: First Meeting

I was 11 when I first joined the kids band at church, just 6 months after my mum and I had moved to One Church. I didn't have many friends to start with but joining Purpose gave me the confidence I needed.

When I was 14 there was a kids band at church, and seeing as I was at church every Wednesday anyway (the night of worship practice), I thought it would be a good idea to join. I want to say it was so I could give back to the church, but honestly, I just wanted girls to think I was cool; turns out, 14 year old me was a genius.

After a few years, I joined the youth band, and remember being terrified. Josh was the worship leader on my first night on rota, and he asked me to sing solo! I said no and avoided him for weeks after.

I first noticed Becki when she joined the youth band, I tried to flirt with her but I think I just scared her off... From then on, I stuck to just singing and playing guitar to look good.

30 May 2014: First Friendships

I always looked up to Josh and his friends, and found his & Phil's comedy shows at youth hilarious (I found out later I was the only one). So, as soon as intimidating Josh moved to America, I pounced and infiltrated the group, finally realising my full potential as a nerd.

The second time I noticed Becki was when I skyped my friends from America whilst they were playing Dungeons and Dragons and noticed they had made friends with not one, but two real, live females (and one of them was really hot). Full of confusion, I returned home after my year studying abroad, and set about discovering why this girl had decided to hang out with my nerdy ass friends. Thus began our friendship.

When Josh first returned from America, I was convinced he was going to think I was lame and kick me out of the friendship group. He even spent the first time we hung out together making fun of my laugh in front of all our friends (it was ridiculous) ! But, it turned out he wasn't too scary after all.

30 May 2014: First Feelings

Becki and I really became good friends after getting to know each other at a nerd gang road trip to Thorpe Park, and shortly after started going to the cinema once a week with a few other friends (Unlimited Club for the winnn) . I always tried to analyse the films after to seem really smart; I couldn't sing in the cinema, what else was I supposed to do?

I really started seeing Josh as an attractive guy rather than the funny weirdo with a good voice when we started the Unlimited Club. When he started wearing smart shirts and blazers, and kept talking to me everyday, I figured he too saw past my loud, hyperactive side and was interested in something more than friendship. Unfortunately, I was an idiot.

First Coffee

One day, when I was feeling particularly brave, I asked Becki out to a catch up over coffee, but due to my awkwardness, I didn't clarify with her what it was. She preceeded to spend the two hours we spent together talking about all the boys she was in love with, so with a heavy heart, I took this as a signal that we were just good friends (ouch), and did my best to be there for her.

I was thrilled to get to hang out with Josh one on one, but for some reason I came down with a bad case of foot in mouth syndrome. I desperately wanted his advice on all of my “problems”, but to get it I ended up pushing him away. I then focused on staying close friends with him and we hung out a few more times after that.

Soulmates

Our First Date

After dating for a while and making various terrible decisions, I decided it was time to focus on loving me and growing myself. That was when Josh asked me out on a date. He had been my best friend for the last year and I had always had a soft spot for him, so I agreed, despite my decision to focus on me.

I took Becki for a walk up Painswick beacon, because she had a car and I was broke, and I sat her down and told her how I felt. Partially because it was eating me up inside, and partially because we had literally talked about everything else under the sun and I had nothing left to say.

I, however, had just come out of a relationship and didn't want to start another one so quickly. I was very shocked and excited at the prospect of dating him, but the timing just wasn't right. We agreed to hang out more and see where it lead us, without giving it a label.

01 December 2015: We Made it Official

Unperturbed, I made sure I spoke to Becki every day to see how she was and waited patiently for the next opportunity. After a month or two, I decided to try my luck and ask her on a real date, this time with money.

Josh soon asked me out on our second date. He took me to Bristol (well, I drove), where we went ice skating and had a meal at Pizza Express. He asked me to be his girlfriend, and we made it official! Every year since we have gone ice skating to celebrate.

22 April 2016: Our First Adventure

Our first road trip together was to Manchester to see my brother and his family. During our trip, we went for a beautiful walk along an old train line, I was absolutely exhausted, but I tried my hardest not to show it so Becki didn't think I was weak. To be fair, I had been giving piggy backs to my two nieces the entire time.

I loved getting to meet the rest of Josh's family in Manchester, especially his two beautiful nieces, who will be walking down the aisle with me (alongside a couple more)! We spent most of our Saturday out on a walk along the canal and old train trecks, during which Josh complained the entire time that his legs hurt. It was a great first adventure for us, and we soon went on many more to places like Warwick, Tourquay and London a couple of times!

05 August 2017: The Engagement

Keeping the engagement a surprise was inordinately difficult, mostly because Becki knew it was coming for months; after all, we had designed the ring together, found a venue, and even starting thinking about dates for the big day. However, somehow, I managed to make a trip to Painswick beacon, where we had our first date, seem blasé.

As soon as Josh suggested going for a walk up Painswick beacon, I knew something was up, but I played the fool all the same. . Once we reached the bench we sat on during our first date, I was dying with excitement. But, instead We walked along the hills until we reached the bench where Josh told me how he felt. He sat at the bench and invited me to play Dungeons and Dragons with him (one of our favourite hobbies).

There was only way I could orchestrate a proposal perfect enough for Becki's unique personality; to have her pull it from the bloody corpse of a beaten enemy in a game of Dungeons and Dragons. As is correct, upon heroically defeating her foe, she set about looting the body, and as I described her finding a small intricate black box, I pulled the very same box from my pocket and placed it on the table in front of her.

When Josh pulled out the small black box from his pocket, I think I actually stopped breathing. Even though we had been planning getting engaged for so long, I was not at all ready for it to happen! I teared up as Josh got down on one knee, looked up at me and asked me to be his bride. I, obviously, said yes and hugged him for so long I might have cut off some of his blood supply, and to this day, it has been the best decision of my life.
